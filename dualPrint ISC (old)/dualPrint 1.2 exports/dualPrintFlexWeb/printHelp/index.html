The main puspouse of dualprint is to facilitate the work of printing works using both sides of the paper.

  1. Start dualPrint. Then choose the page to start printing from.
    This is usually the first page of the document. Yet, you may want to skip titles or introductory slides.

  2. Take a look at the total of pages in the document and choose up to what page will the print go.
    If a document has 50 pages but the last 3 are of irrelevant content, you may just print up to page 47.

  3. Then type in how many pages will be printed per side of a sheet.
    For handouts, one usually wants 4 or 6 pages per side. For books either 1 or 2 pages per side.

  4. Once you're done entering the details on dualPrint, press the “Generate Print Sets” button.

  5. Now copy the Odd print set, the one from the first row.
    In the Desktop and Flash versions of dualPrint this is done automatically.

  6. Now go to your document and access the print dialog. You may do this by pressing Ctrl-P or clicking File - Print.

  7. Look for "range" options in the print dialog.
    One generally selects a 'pages' radio button and manually types which pages to print. Instead of typing them, paste the content that was copied from dualPrint in Step 5 (or 12). To paste it, press Ctrl-V on your keyboard.

  8. Now look for an option to "print multiple pages per sheet" and set the pages per sheet to the same number as dualPrint (see step 3).
    The pages per sheet could be located near a "layout setup" or a "page setup" area. If you're printing an electronic presentation, set the slides per page or handouts instead.

  9. Print the document.

  10. Turn the printed pages' position to print on the back of them.
    To learn how to turn pages on any printer see Turning Pages.

  11. After turning the pages, you must reverse their order too.
    If there's an even total of pages, use the "Reverse (page order)" option available in most print dialogs. If the total of pages is an Odd number, remove the page from the top and then use the "reverse" option.
    Since using the automatic 'Reverse' option may be confusing, you can invert the pages' order manually. Do this by taking each page from the top of the printed stack and placing it on the top of a new stack based on the printed stack.

  12. Remove any unused pages from the print tray you've just used. Load the pages from the last print in the same tray.

  13. Copy the print set from the Even text-box row. These are the pages to print on the back.
    You may use the Copy buttons on the left of the sets to copy them into the clipboard as well.

  14. Repeat steps 6 through 9.

  15. Manually reverse your pages' order as explained in step 11.
