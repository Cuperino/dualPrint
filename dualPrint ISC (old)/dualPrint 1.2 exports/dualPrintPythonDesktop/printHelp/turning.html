Printers come in all sizes and with all sorts of mechanisms. The simplest way to know how to print on both sides of a paper from any printer is to print two documents on a same sheet of paper and use the result as a reference to manage the paper.
  1. Let's go ahead and just do that. Use the “Test Print” buttons for this. Print “Test Print 1” now and keep the paper source tray you choose in mind.



  2. After you're done printing Test Print 1, reinsert the printed page back into the same source tray. Do not flip or rotate it in any way.

    If your printer's source tray and output have a 90° difference, turn the page as you consider most appropriate. Just remember that you must do this page turn, before any other, when printing the real document.

  3. Now do “Test Print 2”

  4. Compare your result with the following table. Depending on this are the rotation or flipping instructions to follow before printing the second side of a document.
1
 2
 3
 4
Flip horizontally
 Flip vertically
 Rotate 180° -> Do nothing.


Notes:

  • Portrait and landscape prints have different results.
    For landscape documents exchange actions 1 with 2 and 3 with 4 correspondingly.

  • It's not advised to exchange trays halve way of a two sided print.
  • Each printing tray uses a different mechanism to manage paper. You may need to repeat the test for each tray of each printer you use.
